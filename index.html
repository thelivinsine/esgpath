What even is ESG?

Think of ESG as a scorecard for how a company behaves — beyond just making money. It answers: is this company good for the planet, fair to people, and run honestly?

E

🌱

Environmental

The Planet

How does a company impact the natural world? Does it pollute? How much carbon does it emit? Does it manage its water use?

  • Carbon footprint (Scope 1, 2 & 3 emissions)
  • Climate risk exposure (floods, heat, fires)
  • Waste, water, and biodiversity
  • Clean energy transition plans
S

🤝

Social

The People

How does the company treat its workers, suppliers, customers, and communities? Are human rights respected?

  • Worker safety & fair pay
  • Diversity, equity & inclusion (DEI)
  • Supply chain labour standards
  • Community impact & data privacy
G

🏛️

Governance

The Rules

Is the company run honestly and transparently? Are its leaders accountable? Does the board act in shareholders' interests?

  • Board independence & diversity
  • Executive pay vs company performance
  • Anti-corruption & ethics
  • Shareholder rights
The Roles

A day in the life

Three very different jobs all wear the ESG badge. Pick a role to see what your Tuesday might actually look like.

📍 You work for a ratings firm like MSCI or Sustainalytics. Today you're rating a large oil & gas company.

  1. 9am

    Pull latest emissions data from CDP database

    Cross-reference the company's self-reported Scope 1 & 2 figures against satellite data and industry benchmarks. Flag any anomalies for follow-up.

    Skill: Data Analysis
  2. 10:30

    Read the company's 200-page Sustainability Report

    Extract disclosure against GRI Standards and SASB Oil & Gas framework. Note what's missing — gaps in disclosure are themselves a red flag for investors.

    Skill: Reporting Frameworks
  3. 1pm

    Update ESG rating model in Excel / Python

    Weight environmental score (40%), social (30%), governance (30%) based on sector exposure. Adjust for a new lawsuit over a pipeline spill — this tanks the Social sub-score.

    Skill: Financial Modelling
  4. 3pm

    Write analyst note for portfolio managers

    "We downgrade ExxonMobil's ESG score from BBB to BB. Key driver: elevated controversy exposure (pipeline incident) and lack of credible short-term emissions targets vs peers."

    Skill: Written Communication
  5. 4:30pm

    Regulatory scan: What does the new CSRD mandate?

    The EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive now requires ~50,000 companies to disclose ESG data. Update methodology to reflect new mandatory disclosure scope starting 2025.

    Skill: Regulatory Knowledge

📍 You work at PwC. Your client: a mid-size European manufacturer facing their first CSRD compliance deadline.

  1. 8:30am

    Client kickoff call — stakeholder mapping

    Meet the CFO, Head of Ops, and Legal team. Build a map of who owns what ESG data internally: HR owns employee data, procurement owns supply chain, finance owns energy bills.

    Skill: Stakeholder Management
  2. 11am

    Double Materiality Assessment workshop

    Facilitate a session to identify which ESG topics matter most: both what impacts the company financially (financial materiality) AND what the company's impact is on the world (impact materiality). Required by CSRD.

    Skill: Facilitation & Frameworks
  3. 2pm

    Gap analysis: current disclosures vs CSRD requirements

    Build a spreadsheet mapping 82 ESRS data points. Client currently discloses 23 of them. Flag 59 gaps — 15 are "critical" (fines risk), 44 are "develop over 2 years".

    Skill: Regulatory Expertise
  4. 4pm

    Build the roadmap deck for board presentation

    Translate the gap analysis into a 3-year action plan with budget, owners, and KPIs. The board cares about: avoiding fines, investor confidence, and "not being in the newspaper for the wrong reasons."

    Skill: Executive Comms

📍 You're Head of ESG at a FTSE 250 retailer. Annual sustainability report is due in 6 weeks.

  1. 9am

    Collect Scope 3 data from 400+ suppliers

    Chase 12 suppliers who haven't submitted emissions data. Scope 3 = your supply chain's carbon. For a retailer, it's typically 90%+ of total emissions. You can't report without it.

    Skill: Supply Chain Management
  2. 10:30am

    Investor call — questions on DEI targets

    A major pension fund asks why female representation on the board dropped from 40% to 33%. You explain the departure and the recruitment plan. Investors want to know you're taking it seriously.

    Skill: Investor Relations
  3. 1:30pm

    Internal review: are we on track to cut emissions 50% by 2030?

    Build progress dashboard. You're at -18% since 2019 baseline. Need to accelerate. Recommend switching distribution fleet to EVs — saves £2.4M/yr AND reduces Scope 1 by 22%.

    Skill: Strategy & Business Case
  4. 3:30pm

    Review draft sustainability report with external auditors

    Third-party assurance is now mandatory. The auditors flag that your water intensity figure can't be verified because the data collection methodology changed mid-year. Fix before publishing.

    Skill: Reporting & Assurance
Real World

Case studies: ESG in action

Here's how ESG professionals actually changed things at real companies you've heard of.

☕

Starbucks

Retail · Environmental

The Problem

Starbucks operated 36,000+ stores globally with no standardised framework to measure or verify environmental performance across them.

What ESG professionals did

Working with WWF and SCS Global Services, ESG consultants developed the "Greener Stores" framework — standardising low-energy equipment, water-saving fixtures, and waste diversion across all locations. Rigorous third-party verification was built in from day one to prevent greenwashing claims.

  • 6,091 stores certified
  • ~30% energy reduction
  • $60M saved annually
⚡

Tesla

Automotive · Carbon Accounting

The Problem

EV companies face unique ESG scrutiny — their products reduce emissions, but what about manufacturing and charging? Investors wanted rigorous, verifiable impact data, not marketing claims.

What ESG professionals did

Tesla's ESG team developed "avoided emissions" methodology — calculating how much CO₂ customers avoided by driving Teslas instead of combustion vehicles. They tracked factory-level renewable energy conversion at Gigafactory Berlin and published a verified annual impact update with third-party data sources.

  • 30M tonnes CO₂ avoided in 2024
  • 100% renewable Superchargers
🏦

HSBC

Banking · Sustainable Finance

The Problem

Banks face a unique ESG challenge: their biggest impact isn't their own offices — it's the companies they lend to and invest in ("financed emissions"). Regulators and investors started demanding accountability for these too.

What ESG professionals did

HSBC's ESG team published a net-zero roadmap covering both operational emissions (targeting neutrality by 2025) and financed emissions (neutrality by 2050). They introduced a 29% absolute reduction target for oil & gas financed emissions by 2030 — the first such target by a global bank — and earned an MSCI AA rating for ESG practices.

  • MSCI AA Rating
  • $982M sustainable finance income
  • Industry-first Scope 3 targets
🛋️

IKEA

Retail · Supply Chain

The Problem

IKEA sources from thousands of suppliers in 50+ countries. How do you ensure child labour, unsafe factories, or illegal logging don't exist somewhere in that vast network?

What ESG professionals did

ESG specialists developed "IWAY" — IKEA's supplier code of conduct now in its 6th version. Suppliers are audited against core worker rights, safety, work-life balance, water/waste management, and child labour prevention. Failing suppliers get a development plan; repeat failures lose IKEA contracts.

  • 20+ years in operation
  • All suppliers must comply
  • Regular independent audits
What You Need to Know

The skills that get you hired

ESG is genuinely interdisciplinary. Here's what employers actually look for — and how hard each is to develop.

  • Data Analysis

    Importance: Essential

    Excel, Python, or R for processing large ESG datasets. You'll constantly clean messy corporate data and spot what's missing or implausible.

  • Financial Literacy

    Importance: Very High

    Understanding P&L statements, risk models, and how ESG factors translate into financial risk or opportunity. Speaks the language of the boardroom.

  • Regulatory Knowledge

    Importance: Very High

    CSRD, SEC climate rules, ISSB standards, EU Taxonomy. You don't need a law degree, but you need to read and understand complex regulatory texts.

  • Reporting Frameworks

    Importance: High

    GRI, SASB, TCFD, CDP, ISSB. Each framework has different metrics and audiences. Knowing when to use which one is a core competency.

  • Climate Science Basics

    Importance: High

    Understanding Scope 1/2/3 emissions, physical climate risk, transition risk, carbon markets, and net-zero concepts. You don't need a PhD — but you need the fundamentals.

  • Written Communication

    Importance: Essential

    Translating complex ESG data into clear, credible reports for investors, boards, and the public. Bad writing = your analysis gets ignored.

Your Roadmap

The career ladder

Here's a realistic path from zero to ESG leader — with salary benchmarks based on real market data.

  1. 0

    Break In — Now

    Before your first ESG job

    You don't need an ESG-specific degree. Backgrounds in finance, environmental science, economics, law, or social science all work. Start building knowledge and proof of interest now — certifications, volunteer work, or a passion project matter more than you think.

    • 📚 CFA Certificate in ESG Investing (~130 hrs)
    • 🎓 GRI Sustainability Reporting course
    • 📰 Follow: CDP, GRI, MSCI ESG Research
    • 💼 Internship at sustainability consultancy
  2. 1

    ESG Analyst / Associate

    Years 0–3

    Your first real role. You're doing the grunt work — collecting data, building models, researching frameworks, writing initial reports. You're learning fast. Common entry points: ESG ratings firms (MSCI, Sustainalytics), consulting firms' sustainability teams, or in-house analyst roles at large companies.

    💵 $62K – $85K / year (US)

    • Build: Excel & Python skills
    • Learn: One sector deeply (e.g. energy, finance)
    • Earn: CESGA or GARP SCR certification
  3. 2

    Senior Analyst / ESG Consultant

    Years 3–7

    You're now leading pieces of projects, mentoring juniors, and building client relationships. If in consulting, you're running workshops and writing strategy decks for boards. If at a ratings firm, you're owning entire sector coverage. Your expertise in one ESG domain (e.g. climate risk, governance) becomes a calling card.

    💵 $85K – $125K / year (US)

    • Specialise: Climate risk, supply chain, or governance
    • Network: Industry conferences (PRI in Person, GreenBiz)
    • Publish: Op-eds, LinkedIn thought leadership
  4. 3

    ESG Manager / Director

    Years 7–12

    You're running a team or a programme. In-house, this is the Head of ESG role — owning the company's entire sustainability strategy, sitting in leadership meetings, presenting to the board. In consulting, you're a client partner managing multiple accounts. The job becomes more about influence, negotiation, and organisational change than analysis.

    💵 $125K – $160K / year (US)

  5. ✦

    Chief Sustainability Officer / ESG Partner

    12+ years

    The top of the ESG ladder. As CSO, you sit at the C-suite table, shaping company strategy, responding to activist investors, and representing the company publicly. ESG is no longer a function — it's embedded in how the whole business operates. Salary ranges widely based on company size.

    💵 $160K – $300K+ / year (US)

How to Get Credible

The certifications worth getting

These are globally recognised and will actually help you get interviews. Pick the one that fits your background.

  • 📘

    CFA Certificate in ESG Investing

    CFA Institute · Global

    The gold standard for finance professionals moving into ESG. Covers ESG integration into portfolio construction, stewardship, and reporting. Requires ~130 hours of self-study. Highly respected at asset managers and banks.

    Best for: Finance backgrounds~$895 exam fee
  • 🌱

    CESGA — Certified ESG Analyst

    EFFAS · European-focused

    Comprehensive across all three ESG pillars, with a strong emphasis on financial integration and ESG ratings methodology. Very well recognised in European markets and consulting firms. Includes modules on climate risk and regulatory frameworks.

    Best for: Analysts & consultants~€700 exam fee
  • 📊

    GARP SCR — Sustainability & Climate Risk

    GARP · Global

    Focused on climate and sustainability risk from a risk management lens. Ideal if you're moving from traditional risk roles (credit, market risk) into ESG. Growing recognition in banking and insurance sectors. Exam has an 81% pass rate.

    Best for: Risk professionals~$475 exam fee
  • 🌿

    GRI Sustainability Reporting

    Global Reporting Initiative

    GRI is the world's most widely used sustainability reporting framework. Their professional learning programmes cover how to build and verify a GRI-aligned report. Practical and immediately applicable — useful if you're going in-house or into consulting.

    Best for: In-house & consultantsFree to ~€300
Where the Jobs Are

Who's actually hiring

ESG talent is demanded across four very different employer ecosystems. Each has its own culture, pay scale, and career trajectory.

Asset Managers & Investors

The original ESG employers. They hire analysts to integrate ESG into investment decisions, run shareholder engagement programmes, and manage sustainable fund products. High pay. Intellectually rigorous. Heavy on financial modelling.

  • BlackRock, Vanguard, Schroders
  • Pension funds & sovereign wealth funds
  • Impact investors (Triodos, Impax)
  • Private equity & real estate firms

Typical Roles

  • ESG Research Analyst
  • Sustainable Investing Specialist
  • Stewardship Analyst

Consulting Firms

Strategy consulting firms have built major sustainability practices. Fast career progression. You'll work across multiple industries and geographies. Great for building broad expertise quickly. Demanding hours, but excellent training.

  • Big 4: PwC, Deloitte, KPMG, EY
  • Strategy: McKinsey, BCG, Bain
  • Specialists: ERM, South Pole, Anthesis
  • Boutiques: Verdantix, Carnstone

Typical Roles

  • ESG Consultant
  • Sustainability Advisor
  • Climate Risk Specialist

Large Corporates (In-House)

As regulation forces disclosure, FTSE 100 and Fortune 500 companies are building full ESG teams. More stable hours than consulting. You own the outcome — not just advise on it. Great for people who want to drive change inside an organisation.

  • Banks, insurers (finance)
  • Energy, utilities, oil & gas
  • Consumer goods, retail, food
  • Real estate, infrastructure, tech

Typical Roles

  • ESG Manager
  • Sustainability Reporting Lead
  • Head of ESG

ESG Data & Ratings Firms

The infrastructure layer of the ESG industry. These firms build the ratings, datasets, and analytics products that everyone else uses. Strong analytical culture. You'll become an expert in methodology and sector coverage. Increasingly using AI.

  • MSCI ESG Research
  • Sustainalytics (Morningstar)
  • S&P Global Sustainable1
  • Bloomberg ESG, ISS, RepRisk

Typical Roles

  • ESG Research Analyst
  • ESG Data Analyst
  • Methodology Specialist
Clearing the Air

Myths about ESG careers

You've probably heard some of these. Let's put them to rest.

Myth

"You need an environmental science degree"

Reality

Finance, economics, law, social science, and even engineering are equally valid entry points. ESG needs people who can speak to investors and boards — financial literacy is often more valued than environmental credentials at entry level.

Myth

"ESG is just box-ticking and greenwashing"

Reality

Regulation is making this harder and harder to get away with. CSRD, SEC climate rules, and ISSB standards require third-party verified data. ESG professionals spend significant effort preventing greenwashing — it's one of the core challenges of the job.

Myth

"ESG is just a trend that's fading"

Reality

Mandatory reporting laws now apply to most large companies in the EU, UK, and increasingly the US. 99% of S&P 500 companies publish ESG reports. Whether or not the term "ESG" stays fashionable, the underlying regulatory and investor requirements are structural and growing.

Myth

"ESG jobs don't pay well"

Reality

Entry-level ESG analysts earn $62K–$85K in the US. Senior managers earn $125K–$160K. CSOs at large companies can earn $200K+. At asset managers and top consulting firms, total compensation is competitive with equivalent non-ESG roles.

Myth

"You need years of experience to get in"

Reality

Entry-level roles exist — but they're competitive. A relevant certification (CFA ESG or CESGA), demonstrable knowledge of one framework (GRI, TCFD), and a focused cover letter can get you there. Boutique sustainability consultancies are often the best first stepping stone.

Myth

"ESG is only about the environment"

Reality

The S (Social) and G (Governance) are equally important — and often under-resourced. Human rights in supply chains, board diversity, pay equity, anti-corruption, and data privacy are all ESG issues. Many specialists build entire careers on the Social or Governance pillars alone.

Ready to start?
Here's your first week.

You don't need to change careers overnight. These five steps will give you genuine traction in ESG — starting today.

  1. 1

    Read one sustainability report cover-to-cover. Start with Apple or Microsoft — they're exceptionally well-written. Notice what data they include and what they leave out.

  2. 2

    Enrol in the CFA Certificate in ESG Investing or the GRI Learning Platform (free intro modules). Get the vocabulary down — it opens doors immediately.

  3. 3

    Follow 5 ESG practitioners on LinkedIn. KPMG's ESG practice, MSCI ESG Research, PRI, and CDP all share excellent explainers. Engage with the content.

  4. 4

    Pick one company you find interesting and write a 500-word ESG analysis. What are their material ESG risks? What are they disclosing well? What's missing? This becomes a portfolio piece.

  5. 5

    Message one person working in ESG on LinkedIn — a genuine question about their day-to-day. Most people in this field are generous with their time. Informational interviews lead to referrals.

"All forms of information are out there — it is just about the means of seeking the knowledge." — KPMG ESG Consultant (on transitioning careers into sustainability)
